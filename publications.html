Elahi, A., Caragea, C., Caragea D. (2024 - Under Preparation) Co-Training as a Domain Adaptation Algorithm for water Quality Prediction in Data-Scarce Locations
Elahi, A., Shumway, D., Caragea, C., Caragea D., Dorevitch S., (2024 - Under Review) Leveraging Readily Available Web Data to Predict Surface Water Bacteria Levels Using Domain Adaptation [Download]

Abstract: [Show]


We use Random Forest (RF), extreme gradient boosting (XGBoost), and attentive interpretable tabular learning (TabNet) models to establish strong baselines in supervised learning and transfer learning settings on our Chicago beaches water quality dataset. We further show that applying supervised and unsupervised domain adaptation methods enhances transfer learning performance. Specifically, our results show that the supervised methods, especially RF, are very promising and that domain adaptation such as Feature Augmentation and Ballance Weighting could be successfully employed to model FIB levels in settings where FIB levels are rarely measured.

Elahi, A., Zirak, A. (2021). A Comparative Study of Recommendation Algorithms: Online and Offline Evaluations on a Large-scale Recommender System [Download]

Abstract: [Show]


This study examines a large-scale recommender system operating on approximately 100 websites, handling around 300 requests per second over the previous year. This system demonstrates user-based and item-based recommendations employing algorithms structured upon content-based (CB), collaborative filtering (CF), trend-based, and hybridization of those. Our methods of evaluation include offline tests including accuracy and ranking metrics like hit-rate@k and nDCG, and online tests consisting of click-through rate (CTR) and page per session (PPS). Additionally, the study addresses challenges like popularity bias and cold start issues, detailing the strategies employed to mitigate these problems.
