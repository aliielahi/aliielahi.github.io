Leveraging Readily Available Web Data to Predict Surface Water Bacteria Levels Using Domain Adaptation[Download]
Submitted (The Web Conference)

Abstract:
Surface water contaminated by fecal bacteria can cause diarrheal illness, threatening human health (especially among children). In this paper, we introduce a water quality dataset collected from beaches in Chicago, USA, and present strong ML baselines to predict historical FIB levels on this dataset. Specifically, we use Random Forest (RF), extreme gradient boosting (XGBoost), and attentive interpretable tabular learning (TabNet) models to establish strong baselines in supervised learning and transfer learning settings on our Chicago beaches dataset. We further show that applying supervised and unsupervised domain adaptation methods enhances transfer learning performance in general. Specifically, our results show that the supervised methods, especially RF, are very promising and that domain adaptation could be successfully employed to model FIB levels in settings where FIB levels are rarely measured.

A Comparative Study of Recommendation Algorithms: Online and Offline Evaluations on a Large-scale Recommender System [Download]
2021

Abstract:
Recommender Systems are one of the most common Artificial Intelligence applications used to aid users in finding relevant items efficiently. A recommender system's performance, in a practical view, is coupled with the satisfaction of users and providers. Satisfaction, however, is not easily transformable into a mathematical optimization problem and thus, not represented by offline metrics such as accuracy and diversity. Although most state-of-the-art studies assess their accuracy with offline tests and metrics, many scholars have claimed that in today's marketplace, online tests such as A/B testing are more suitable in this regard.
On the other hand, most recent studies only emphasize the utilization of recommender systems in circumstantial environments, discerning them from the wide-reaching nature of the present study. We have employed a variety of algorithms on different types of datasets divergent in size and subject, producing recommendations in various platforms, including media streaming services, digital publishing websites, e-commerce systems, and news broadcasting networks.
